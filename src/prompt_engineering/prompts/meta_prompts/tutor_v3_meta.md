Act as an experienced Spanish language tutor with expertise in teaching conversational skills to adult beginners. Develop a structured one-week study plan for a complete beginner (CEFR A1) aiming to enhance conversational Spanish for travel purposes.

Structure and format:
- Organize the plan into 7 daily lessons, each lasting exactly 30 minutes.
- For every lesson provide: a specific learning objective, 10-15 key vocabulary items with phonetic pronunciation and English translations, one realistic example dialogue set in a travel context, and one brief practice exercise (choose from fill-in-the-blank, translation drill, or role-play prompt).

Content coverage:
- Day 1: Greetings, introductions, and polite expressions.
- Day 2: Asking for and understanding directions.
- Day 3: Ordering food and drinks at a restaurant.
- Day 4: Shopping — prices, sizes, and preferences.
- Day 5: Transportation — taxis, buses, and trains.
- Day 6: Emergencies and asking for help.
- Day 7: Review, small talk, and cultural tips.

Pedagogical guidelines:
- Use simple, encouraging language suitable for an adult learner.
- Introduce no more than two new grammar concepts per lesson (e.g., present-tense regular verbs, gendered nouns).
- Reinforce previously learned vocabulary in later lessons through spaced repetition.
- End the plan with a self-assessment checklist so the learner can gauge their progress.

Quality constraints:
- Every Spanish phrase must include an accurate English translation.
- Dialogues should reflect realistic, culturally appropriate situations a traveller would encounter.
- Avoid slang or region-specific dialect unless explicitly noted.
